How to Play Skud Pai Sho

A two-player strategy game on a circular board. Plant flowers, form harmonies, and close a ring around the center to win.

The Goal

Be the first player to form a Harmony Ring — a connected chain of your own harmonies that fully encloses the center of the board. Two other endings exist (last basic flower planted, forfeit) and are covered below.

The Board & Your Tiles

  • The board is a circle inside a 19×19 grid with four Gates on the cardinal edges (N, S, E, W). Flowers enter play through your Gates.
  • The playing surface is split into Red, White, and Neutral gardens. A red flower may only rest on red or neutral gardens; a white flower only on white or neutral. The center space itself cannot be occupied.
  • Each player's set contains six Basic Flowers (three red: Rose, Chrysanthemum, Rhododendron — three white: Jasmine, Lily, Jade), two Special Flowers (White Lotus, Orchid), and four Accent Tiles (Rock, Wheel, Knotweed, Boat).

The Circle of Harmony

The six basic flowers relate to each other on a circle. Adjacent flowers can form a harmony with each other. Flowers opposite on the circle (same number, opposite colour) clash.

Your Turn

On each turn, take one of the following actions:

  • Plant — place a basic flower from your reserve onto one of your own open Gates.
  • Arrange — move a basic or special flower already on the board in a straight line (no diagonals, no jumping over tiles) up to its movement range.

A flower is Blooming once it has left its Gate and is standing on a legal garden space. Only blooming flowers participate in harmonies and most interactions. Freshly-planted flowers on their Gate are growing, not yet blooming.

Harmonies

Two of your blooming basic flowers form a Harmony when:

  • They are neighbours on the Circle of Harmony (see diagram above), and
  • They share the same row or column, with nothing between them (no flowers, accent tiles, or disabling effects).

Harmonies belong to you and appear on the board as a coloured line linking the two tiles.

Harmony Bonus

Whenever you create a new harmony on your turn (through a plant or an arrange), you immediately receive a bonus action: plant an accent tile from your reserve, or place one of your special flowers (White Lotus / Orchid) onto one of your Gates. You only get one bonus per new harmony created; forming multiple harmonies in one turn does not stack.

Clashes

Two basic flowers clash when they sit opposite each other on the Circle of Harmony (same number, opposite colour: Rose↔Jasmine, Chrysanthemum↔Lily, Rhododendron↔Jade). Clashing flowers cannot form a harmony on a line that runs through the other, regardless of who owns them.

Clash Traps

A clash trap happens when a flower ends up on a line with an opposing clash partner such that it can't move without colliding further. Use clashes to block your opponent's intended harmony lines.

Captures

You may capture an opposing basic flower by ending a move on its space, but only if the captured flower is not currently in a harmony. Harmonised flowers are protected. Captured flowers are removed from the board and returned to the opposing player's reserve.

Accent Tiles

Accent tiles are placed as a Harmony Bonus. Each accent has its own placement rules and effect:

  • Rock — an immovable obstacle. Any flower on one of the four grid lines passing through the rock cannot form harmonies along that line. Cannot be placed on a gate or the center.
  • Wheel — when placed, it rotates the eight surrounding tiles one step clockwise around it. Tiles that can't legally move stay put.
  • Knotweed — disables harmonies for the eight flowers adjacent to it (including diagonals). It does not move them, only mutes them.
  • Boat — placed on top of an existing flower or accent. When placed on a flower, you may move that flower to any legal space on the board. When placed on an accent, it simply removes it. The Boat is consumed afterwards.

Special Flower Tiles

Special flowers are not in your starting reserve in play; you deploy them via a Harmony Bonus (place onto one of your open Gates, like planting a basic flower).

White Lotus

Moves up to 2 spaces. The White Lotus may form a harmony with any basic flower on the same row/column (with nothing in between), regardless of whom the basic flower belongs to. A harmony that involves an opponent's basic flower still belongs to the owner of that basic flower — use carefully.

Orchid

Moves up to 6 spaces — the longest range in the game. The Orchid traps every opposing basic flower on the four squares orthogonally adjacent to it (those flowers cannot move). While a blooming White Lotus is on the board, every Orchid temporarily becomes wild: it can stand on any garden colour.

Winning the Game

The game ends by one of three conditions (checked in this order):

  1. Harmony Ring — a connected loop of your own harmonies that fully encloses the center space. The player who completes it wins immediately.
  2. Last Basic Flower — the moment a player plants their last basic flower onto the board (their basic-flower reserve becomes empty), the game ends. The winner is the player with more midline-crossing harmonies — harmonies whose two tiles lie on opposite sides of one of the four lines that divide the board into quadrants (row 9 and column 9). If both players have the same count, the game is a tie.
  3. Forfeit — a player may resign at any time.
Tip: Harmonies are worth more than captures. Plan your ring around the center from your very first plant — the opening Gate you choose decides the geometry of the whole game.
